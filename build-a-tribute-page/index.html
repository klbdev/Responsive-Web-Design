A brief history of Sir Isaac Newton

About Isaac Newton

  • 4 Jan 1643 - 31 Mar 1727
  • Polymath, mathematician, physicist, astronomer, alchemist, theologian, and author
  • Known for his contribution to Physics in the Newton Laws of Motion
  • Education: Trinity College, Cambridge

Laws of Motion

1. First Law of Inertia

An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in motion at a constant velocity in a straight line in the absence of an external resultant force.

2. Second Law F = dp/dt

The rate of change of momentum of a body is directly proportional to the resultant force acting on the body and occurs in the same direction of that force.

3. Third Law of action-reaction

If body A exerts a force on body B, then body B exerts a force equal in magnitude, of the same type and in the opposite direction on body A.
